🏨 Amenitiz · Head of Design & Research · Sep 2023–Nov 2024

Expanding a hospitality SaaS platform to 60+ countries

RoleHead of Design & Research
Team6 designers hired in 45 days
Scale60+ countries · 15k+ properties
FocusInternational expansion, PLG, LTV

The situation

Amenitiz had strong product-market fit in its core European markets but needed to scale internationally — across 60+ countries and 15,000+ properties — without degrading the experience that made it successful in the first place.

There was no research practice, no localization framework, and no dedicated design leadership. I was brought in to build those foundations and lead the product transformation required to support international scale.

What I did

I recruited a full team of six designers in my first 45 days — which required running a hiring process, defining roles and standards, and onboarding people into an environment without established design systems or documentation. Rather than waiting until the team was settled, I put everyone to work immediately on an innovation workshop with Product and Engineering to align on priorities and build shared context fast.

Before touching the product, I led three strategic research trips to key accounts — visiting properties directly to understand how hospitality operators actually worked across different markets. That research informed everything that followed and gave the team a shared grounding in user reality that you can't get from internal data alone.

I then led the redesign of Amenitiz's core UIs: calendar, self-service onboarding, and inventory management — the three surfaces that determined whether a new property could get up and running independently. The redesigns were built to scale across languages, property types, and operational contexts.

I personally led the Brazilian market launch: new language, personalized product experience, and a streamlined self-service signup flow designed for a lower-LTV prospect profile and a full PLG motion. No sales-assisted onboarding — the product had to do the work on its own.

I also established the company's first user research and localization best practices — repeatable frameworks that gave the team a foundation to work from after I left.

What I learned

Moving fast in a new role doesn't mean skipping the research. The three field trips to customer accounts were the single best investment I made in those early months. They surfaced problems that no analytics dashboard would have shown, and they built instant credibility with the product and engineering teams who saw that design was willing to get its hands dirty.

The Brazilian launch was also a useful forcing function: designing a PLG motion for a new market — with no sales support and a different economic profile — requires making every friction point in onboarding visible and eliminating it. That discipline made the core product better for everyone.

Outcomes

+20% LTV & retention — NPS held flat through the transition
6 designers hired and onboarded in first 45 days
60+ countries supported via redesigned core UIs
1 new market launched (Brazil) with full PLG motion
