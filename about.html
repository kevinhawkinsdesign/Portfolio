I'm Kevin Hawkins — a VP of Product Experience based in Barcelona, with 20+ years working at the intersection of behavioral science, data, and design. I've led teams as large as 105 across design, research, content, and localization — and my work is measured in revenue, retention, and the quality of decisions it enables.

I came up through the craft. Early on I was building web products, doing data visualization for The Brookings Institution, and consulting for organizations like the Federal Reserve and PwC. That technical and analytical foundation shapes everything I do as a leader — I'm never far from the data, and I'm comfortable bridging design and business in the same conversation.

How I lead

My job as a leader is to create the conditions where talented people do the best work of their careers. That means setting a sharp vision, delegating real ownership, and staying close enough to the work to lead by example — running research sprints, facilitating cross-functional workshops, writing the first draft of a design system principles doc. I don't manage from a distance.

At Glovo, I joined a Barcelona-based team of 65 and grew it to 105 across five disciplines, building the research practice and org structure from scratch while shipping product used across 25 countries. At Amenitiz, I recruited a full team of six designers in my first 45 days and immediately put them to work on a product redesign that increased LTV and retention by 20%. At Monta, I joined as the company's first UX leader and formalized the entire function from scratch — vision, playbook, career framework, and all.

I'm data-oriented in a specific way: I care about the metrics that actually predict business outcomes — MAU growth, funnel conversion, LTV, retention cohorts — and I build the experimentation infrastructure to track them. At Monta, I owned the PostHog implementation personally and drove a 20% month-over-month lift in monthly active users through systematic A/B testing and PLG-focused onboarding redesign.

On building diverse teams

As a gay, Liberian-American who has lived and worked across the US, Europe, and Asia, I've long believed that the most effective product decisions come from teams that reflect the people they design for. Building inclusive teams isn't an HR initiative — it's a design quality issue. Every hiring and mentorship decision I make is shaped by this.

Beyond the work

I teach. I've built and delivered UX curriculum at GrowthTribe reaching 5,000+ students, guest-lectured at Georgetown's McCourt School of Public Policy, and co-organize the Hatch Conference Leadership Ateliers — an annual series for 120 UX and product leaders across Europe. Design leadership is a fundamentally different job, and most people have to figure it out alone. I try to change that.

Outside work, I founded The Common Thread Club — a 1,000+ member foodie community in Barcelona built around one-night-only dinners, intimate group trips, and conversations with interesting people. It started as a side project and became one of the things I'm most proud of building.
