🛵 Glovo · Global UX Director · Feb 2022–Sep 2023

Growing a Barcelona-based design org from 65 to 105 — shipping product used across 25 countries

RoleGlobal UX Director
Team65 → 105, based in Barcelona
Product reach25 countries · 19 languages
FocusAcquisition, retention, MAU, cost efficiency

The situation

After Glovo's acquisition by Delivery Hero, I joined as Global UX Director to lead the Barcelona-based product design organization — 65 people at the time, spanning UX designers, researchers, motion designers, UX writers, and localization specialists. The product served users across 25 countries and 19 languages, but the team was operating without a unified research practice or clear design accountability tied to business outcomes.

The mandate was to grow the org, build the research infrastructure, and position design as a driver of Glovo's post-acquisition growth story — not just a production service.

What I did

I grew the Barcelona team from 65 to 105, hiring across all five disciplines while restructuring around Glovo's key revenue areas. Rather than a centralized service model, I built a federated structure where designers were embedded in product squads and held accountable for business metrics, not just delivery.

I built the company's first UX research practice from the ground up — introducing dogfooding rituals to identify competitive gaps firsthand, and establishing quarterly research cycles that fed directly into roadmap decisions. I also led two company-wide hackathons with the CPO and CEO, rapidly prototyping new consumer features: homepage redesign, social shopping, shared payment flows, loyalty program redesign, and product catalogue discovery.

I drove CRM and promotions personalization across the consumer app — a multi-month initiative coordinated across product, engineering, and data science — delivering a 15% lift in new customer acquisition and 20% improvement in retention without degrading LTV.

I also led a company-wide font replacement and localization overhaul — replacing licensed fonts with an open alternative across all 19 languages, with a 24-hour turnaround SLA. That single initiative saved €500k annually in licensing costs while improving brand consistency globally. I simultaneously renegotiated the design tooling stack, cutting a further €500k in annual software spend.

What I learned

Leading a team through a post-acquisition period means managing two things at once: the external work of shipping product that moves the business, and the internal work of rebuilding cohesion and shared purpose after ownership changes. The two are inseparable — teams that don't trust the direction ship slowly, and teams that don't ship lose trust in the direction.

Growing from 65 to 105 also made clear that hiring quality has a direct multiplier effect on everything else. Every person I brought in raised or lowered the bar for the whole org — and that forced a level of deliberateness in hiring decisions that I've carried into every role since.

The hackathons were one of the most effective alignment tools I used: they forced cross-functional collaboration, surfaced the team's best ideas quickly, and gave people genuine pride in what they were building — all in two days.
