✈️ Booking.com · UX Team Lead · Jul 2019–Feb 2021

Cross-brand product innovation at one of the world's largest travel platforms

RoleUX Team Lead
Business unitsConnected Stay, Cross-brand
BrandsBooking.com · Kayak · Priceline · Agoda
LocationAmsterdam

The situation

Booking Holdings owns some of the world's most-used travel platforms — Booking.com, Kayak, Priceline, Agoda, and others. I led two product teams focused on Connected Stay (a cross-product initiative to create a seamless experience across the trip lifecycle) and cross-brand initiatives that required coordination between separate engineering and product organizations across sister companies.

The challenge of cross-brand work at that scale is mostly organizational: getting multiple teams with different priorities, tech stacks, and timelines to converge on something shippable. Design is often the connective tissue that makes that possible.

What I did

I led my teams through two internal hackathons, turning raw product ideas into shippable concepts with clear business cases. The winning concepts — a group travel planner and local events hotel search — both went into production track, demonstrating how design-led experimentation could compress the time from insight to decision at a company that size.

For the cross-brand work, I built the relationships across Kayak, Priceline, and Agoda that made collaboration realistic rather than theoretical — establishing shared design principles and communication channels that let multiple teams move toward the same goal without constant alignment overhead.

I also used this period to sharpen my approach to measuring design impact in a large, highly data-driven organization. At Booking.com, every design decision was expected to have a testable hypothesis and measurable outcome. That rigor shaped how I've approached experimentation ever since.

What I learned

At a company with Booking.com's data culture, design credibility comes from speaking the language of statistical significance and conversion rates — not just user empathy. The best designers I worked with there were fluent in both. That was the bar I held my team to, and it's shaped how I hire and develop designers ever since.

Outcomes

2× hackathon wins — both concepts went to production track
4 brands aligned on cross-platform design principles
2 new consumer features shipped: group planner + local events search
