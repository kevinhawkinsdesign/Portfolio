The challenge

A 60-person team that had never
had a director.

When I joined Glovo, the UX org was large but rudderless. 60 designers, researchers, and writers spread across Europe and Latin America with no director-level leadership for 18 months. Morale was low, processes were inconsistent, and the product hadn't seen a significant consumer UX refresh in years.

Meanwhile, a global headcount reduction was coming. I had to simultaneously stabilize and inspire the team, execute a research org centralization, and ship meaningful product work — all within the same 18 months.

What I led

Four major initiatives
running in parallel.

1. Global User Research Centralization. We downsized the 32-member research team as part of a global headcount cut and rebuilt it with 9 senior and staff researchers leading evergreen research. I implemented RallyUXR and Qualtrics to automate research operations, and established UXR labs in every EMEA regional office for in-market product validation.

2. Homepage & Navigation Overhaul. Based on user insights and heavy competitive benchmarking, we executed a holistic refresh of the information architecture across the consumer app, web, and 3rd party APIs. This included new nomenclature, iconography hierarchies, subnavigation patterns, and a side-of-social-stickiness layer that TechCrunch covered at launch in October 2024.

3. Brand Advertising Product. I led the design of a new consumer-targeted advertising product — purpose-built for 3rd party retailers and global brands to advertise within Glovo's digital real estate. This required moving from a shared admin space into a dedicated, self-serve ads management tool. The result: a Disney·Pixar partnership that became a flagship case and 5× return on ad spend.

4. Career Progression Revamp. I completed a full audit of roles, responsibilities, and the titling framework for designers, researchers, research ops, design ops, and localization specialists — creating a legible career ladder for a 100+ person org for the first time.

Consumer app results

From stagnant to refreshed —
now live in production.

The homepage redesign shipped to production in October 2024. Key wins from the launch:

  • New in-progress order status UI on iPhone — led to 18% fewer contacts to support and 7% fewer no-show deliveries (missed deliveries while customers were in the shower).
  • New motion design, in-app translations, and subnavigation — reduced cognitive load and improved navigation efficiency across all major user segments.
  • New grocery shopping UI, tipping experience, and rebrand of the loyalty program — increased cross-category conversion.
  • Market share gains in Africa — new homepage architecture made the product more accessible and relevant in key growth markets.

"Glovo adds a side of social stickiness to its food delivery app" — TechCrunch, October 2024

Research org

Rebuilding research from the ground up.

After the headcount reduction, I rebuilt the research org with 9 senior and staff researchers — each owning an evergreen research domain rather than being embedded in a single product squad. This model gave us wider coverage with fewer people and produced higher-quality, more strategic insights.

I also launched research.glovoapp.com — a passive feedback collection portal — and recreated the research prioritization process in direct collaboration with the COO, CPO, and Product Directors.

People & culture

Leading a team that hadn't
felt led in 18 months.

The human challenge was as important as the product challenge. I established clarity and certainty across the org: designer-specific onboarding, guest AMAs for professional development, structured bi-weekly 1:1s, and first-hand end-user exposure for every team member.

By practicing the company's core values in daily communication, I upskilled team autonomy and the trust received from stakeholders. My north star: distribute responsibility according to strengths, codify and democratize design playbooks that scale.
