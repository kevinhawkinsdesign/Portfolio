The challenge

A product used by thousands —
never designed with them.

When I arrived, Amenitiz had a powerful product but a weak UX foundation. The interface had grown organically over years of engineering-led development. Hotel staff found it hard to learn, slow to use, and difficult to train others on. The information architecture had 7 unnecessary top-level pages and 3 redundant page layouts.

My mandate: build a design and research practice, establish observational research with real hoteliers, and ship meaningful product improvements — fast.

Research foundation

Starting with the humans
behind the hotels.

Before touching any UI, I ran observational research sessions directly with hotel owners and front-desk staff across multiple countries. These sessions surfaced new personas that were so resonant with our users we had them proudly displayed in the Amenitiz lobby.

I also introduced service blueprints to map pain points across all major user flows — giving the product and engineering teams a shared language for where the experience was breaking down. This became the research council's foundation for quarterly prioritization.

I worked directly with the CEO to communicate product updates to customers, building trust and a direct feedback loop that accelerated our iteration cycles.

Homepage redesign

Eliminating 7 pages.
Making the product trainable.

The homepage and navigation redesign was our first major ship. Using card sorting, 5-second testing, and first-click testing across our core user segments, we restructured the information architecture to match how hoteliers actually think about their work — not how developers had organized features.

  • Eliminated 7 top-level pages and 3 unique page layouts — making the product easier to use, faster to learn, and better for training hotel staff transitioning from legacy systems.
  • 20% improvement in first-click testing from navigation UI changes combined with copy and content iterations.
  • 15% reduction in time-on-task — better time to value for new users.
  • 12% reduction in total pages by merging content based on perceived intent and relevance found in user testing.

Reservations calendar

The #1 feature —
rebuilt in React 18.

The reservations calendar is the most-used feature in the entire product. Hotel staff live in it all day. We redesigned it from scratch — not just visually, but architecturally — refactoring the front-end in React 18 to dramatically improve performance.

User satisfaction scores showed a 30% increase in early prototype testing, and 50% faster loading times after refactoring to React 18. Better mobile responsiveness was an additional bonus.

PriceAdvisor AI

Turning manual pricing
into an AI-powered insight.

Hotel pricing is one of the most time-consuming parts of running an independent property. Before PriceAdvisor, hotel managers manually tracked competitor prices and updated rates across all room types, rate plans, and discounts/promos every day.

I led the UX for PriceAdvisor — an AI-powered feature that automated competitor price tracking and surfaced recommended nightly rates. The before/after was stark: what previously took manual updates across dozens of combinations was now handled automatically, with a clean data visualization showing pricing context and confidence.

4 hours saved per week per property on manual price management. We then continued to iterate — v2 simplified the data visualization further based on ongoing user research, making the insight even easier to act on.

Team & practice

Building the practice
while shipping the product.

In parallel with all product work, I hired 6 designers in 45 days, established the team's ways of working, created a research ops foundation, and put in place the career progression frameworks that gave every designer a clear growth path.

I owned OKRs related to user satisfaction driven by quarterly surveys tracking Customer Effort Score, Customer Health Score, Contact Rate, and QXScore — giving the team a clear, quantified measure of design impact that fed directly into company-level planning.
