Company advisory

I back early-stage founders building what's next.

Product, brand, and design advisory for founders at the earliest, messiest stage, with a particular soft spot for women and Black founders building in tech.

Why I do this

It started before advising was a job title.

In 2017 I was the first designer and employee at Hello Alice (then Circular Board): the first virtual accelerator built for women entrepreneurs. In its first two years the platform helped founders raise over $20M+ in capital, built a community of 13,000+ female founders across six continents, and was named the fastest-growing accelerator for women and minorities.

A year later I co-founded QReview with my best friend: a platform giving people actionable, honest feedback on how inclusive local businesses actually were. We envisioned a world where anyone felt welcomed regardless of how they identify. It ran in 20 countries, in 14 languages, and got covered by The Huffington Post, Daily Dot, and Bustle.

"I'm especially motivated to help women and minorities transition into tech careers."

That's been true of my mentoring for years, and it's just as true of the companies I choose to advise. I look for founders solving real problems in underserved markets, and I care, specifically, about who's in the room building it. Representation isn't a nice-to-have in the product; it changes what gets built and who it works for.

Today that shows up as hands-on product, brand, and design advisory for a small number of early-stage teams: not a portfolio of logos, but a handful of founders I actually talk to.
