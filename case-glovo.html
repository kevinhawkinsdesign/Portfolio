🛵 Glovo · Global UX Director · Feb 2022–Sep 2023

Scaling a 100-person design org across 25 markets post-acquisition

RoleGlobal UX Director
Team100+ across 5 disciplines
Scope25 markets · 19 languages
FocusAcquisition, retention, MAU, cost efficiency

The situation

After Glovo's acquisition by Delivery Hero, the product design organization needed to operate at a significantly higher level of strategic coherence. The org was 100+ people — UX designers, researchers, motion designers, UX writers, and localization specialists — spread across 25 markets, working against fragmented priorities and without a unified research practice.

I was brought in to lead the transformation: align design accountability with revenue outcomes, build the research infrastructure that had been missing, and position UX as a driver of Glovo's post-acquisition growth story.

What I did

I restructured the org to pair design ownership directly with Glovo's key revenue areas — acquisition, retention, and marketplace growth. Rather than a centralized service model, I built a federated structure where designers were embedded in product squads and accountable for business metrics, not just delivery.

I built the company's first global UX research practice from the ground up — introducing dogfooding rituals in key markets to identify competitive gaps firsthand, and establishing quarterly research cycles that fed directly into roadmap decisions. I also led two company-wide hackathons with the CPO and CEO, rapidly prototyping new consumer experiences: homepage redesign, social shopping, shared payment flows, loyalty program redesign, and product catalogue discovery.

I drove CRM and promotions personalization across the consumer app — a multi-month initiative that required coordination across product, engineering, and data science, and delivered a 15% lift in new customer acquisition and 20% improvement in retention without degrading LTV.

I also led a company-wide font replacement and localization overhaul — replacing licensed fonts with an open alternative across all 19 languages and 25 markets, with a 24-hour turnaround SLA. That single initiative saved €500k annually in licensing costs and materially improved brand consistency across markets. I simultaneously renegotiated the design tooling stack and cut a further €500k in software spend.

What I learned

Leading a 100-person org through a post-acquisition period means managing two things simultaneously: the external work of shipping product that moves the business, and the internal work of rebuilding team cohesion and shared purpose after ownership changes. The two are inseparable. Teams that don't trust the direction ship slowly, and teams that don't ship lose trust.

The hackathons were one of the best tools I've used for that: they forced cross-functional collaboration, surfaced the team's best ideas quickly, and gave people a reason to be proud of what they were building — all in two days.
