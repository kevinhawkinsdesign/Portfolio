Context

What BookClub is — and what it required

BookClub is a platform that brings authors and readers together through intimate, author-led online book clubs. Authors guide readers through their own books via video series, live Q&As, public book discussions, and the opportunity to join or host small reading groups. Celebrity hosts — including Gary Vaynerchuk, Hasan Minhaj, Barbara Corcoran, and Charlamagne tha God — led some of the most popular clubs.

Coming in as the founding designer, we had the rare opportunity — and the significant challenge — of designing the product from the ground up. There was no existing design system, no research repository, no established design culture. Everything had to be built: the visual language, the information architecture, the interaction model, the research practice, and the cross-functional rituals that kept the team aligned.

BookClub — "Inspired by Books. Made for the People Who Love Them."
Design Challenge

Intimate yet scalable — the core design tension

The central design tension was intimacy at scale. BookClub needed to feel personal — like a small, curated gathering with your favourite author — while simultaneously supporting thousands of concurrent readers, multi-format media (video, text, audio), and a community layer with comments, reactions, and discussion threads.

We resolved this through careful information architecture that separated discovery (finding clubs and books that match your taste), participation (watching, reading, and discussing within a club), and community (connecting with other readers who share your interests). The navigation model had to serve both the casual browser and the deeply engaged member without overwhelming either.

Production & High-Profile Content

Designing for A-list authors and celebrity hosts

One of the most distinctive aspects of the BookClub product was the caliber of its content partners. The platform hosted video productions featuring bestselling authors and high-profile personalities — each with their own production style, audience expectations, and brand considerations. The design needed to give each club a distinct feeling of identity without creating a fragmented product experience.

We designed flexible content templates that could be branded per author or series while maintaining a consistent underlying structure. The production work — including on-set photography and video direction — fed directly into the design system, ensuring the UI frames and the content within it felt cohesive.
