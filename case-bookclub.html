📚 BookClub · Director of Product Design · Oct 2020–Feb 2022

Building a social reading platform from the ground up

RoleDirector of Product Design
StageFounding designer → Director
ModelTwo-sided: authors + readers
Focus0→1, community, engagement

The situation

BookClub had a genuinely novel idea: author-led online book clubs where readers could join live discussions, Q&A sessions, and video series directly with the authors themselves — and celebrity hosts. The model created a two-sided product: you needed to design for readers who came for the books, and for authors who needed tools to host and engage their communities.

I joined as the founding designer, which meant I was responsible for translating the product vision into actual experience — without an existing design system, research practice, or design team.

What I did

I built the product design and research function from zero. That meant establishing design principles, creating a component library, defining the research cadence, and doing a significant amount of the initial design work myself before growing the team.

The core design challenge was emotional engagement. Readers weren't coming to BookClub to complete a task — they were coming for connection: with authors, with the ideas in a book, and with other readers. That required a fundamentally different design approach than the growth-oriented, conversion-focused products I'd worked on before. Less funnel thinking, more community thinking.

I worked closely with founders and engineers in a fast-moving environment where design decisions had immediate product consequences. I also made the early case for user research as a product investment — not a luxury — and established the research practice before the team expected it to exist.

As the platform grew, I transitioned from individual contributor to Director — hiring and developing the design team, setting standards, and shifting my focus toward product strategy and team health while maintaining quality across the whole experience.

What I learned

Designing for emotional engagement and community is a different discipline than designing for conversion. The metrics that matter — return visits, session depth, community participation — require patience and qualitative understanding that pure A/B testing doesn't give you. This role deepened my appreciation for mixed-method research and for designing with retention in mind from day one.

Being a founding designer also taught me something about the value of speed over perfection in early-stage products: the best version of a feature you shipped last week is almost always better than the perfect version you're still designing.
