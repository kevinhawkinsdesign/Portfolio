The situation

Glovo had recently been acquired by Delivery Hero and needed to accelerate growth across B2C and B2B products in 25 markets. The UX org was fragmented — different teams running different processes, no unified research practice, and design functioning more as a production service than a strategic partner.

What we built

We rebuilt the org from the inside — unifying standards, tooling, and processes across 25 markets while preserving the speed that made individual teams effective. I led two company-wide hackathons with the CPO and CEO to rapidly iterate on homepage, order tracking, social shopping, shared payment, product catalogue discovery, and loyalty program features.

We implemented personalization in CRM and promotions, boosting new customer acquisition by 15% and retention by 20% while preserving LTV. One of our most impactful operational moves: we led a universal font replacement rebranding project that saved €500k EUR in annual expenses — a direct P&L contribution that earned the design function a standing seat in business reviews.

We also overhauled localization and internationalization as an in-house agency supporting 19 languages with 24-hour turnaround times — a capability that directly enabled market-by-market growth.

What I learned

Running a 100-person org across 25 markets taught me that the most important thing a design leader can do isn't design — it's create clarity. Clear ownership, clear standards, clear connection between design decisions and business outcomes. Without that, even excellent individual work gets lost in organizational noise.
