Context

Building fast without sacrificing quality

Amenitiz is an all-in-one hospitality management platform serving independent hotels and properties across 60+ countries. When we joined, the company had strong product-market fit and an ambitious growth trajectory — but no dedicated design leadership. The brief was clear: come in, build a team, and immediately begin improving the product experiences that drive acquisition, onboarding, and retention.

Within 45 days we had recruited a full team of 6 designers and facilitated a cross-functional innovation workshop with Product and Engineering leadership — resetting the team's working norms, aligning on a shared design vision, and identifying the highest-impact areas to tackle first.

Amenitiz Hospitality Management Platform — Property & Calendar Redesign
Core Work

Redesigning the heart of the product

The platform's core UI — the property calendar, self-service onboarding flow, and inventory management — was functional but not optimized for scale or ease of use across diverse property types and markets. We ran a systematic redesign across all three areas, anchored in research findings from three strategic on-site visits with key accounts in different regions.

The calendar redesign alone improved time-on-task for property managers by reducing the number of steps required for common actions. The self-service onboarding overhaul was particularly impactful — enabling the team to scale to new markets without proportionally scaling the customer success team, a critical lever for unit economics at Amenitiz's stage.

Market Expansion

Launching Brazil — a full PLG motion

One of our most strategically complex deliverables was the Brazil market launch. Brazil represented a meaningful expansion opportunity but with different LTV characteristics than Amenitiz's core European markets — meaning the product and acquisition motion needed to be calibrated accordingly.

We designed and delivered a full PLG self-service signup experience specifically for the Brazilian market: localized in Portuguese, personalized to local property management workflows, and streamlined to reduce the onboarding drop-off that was more pronounced in lower-LTV market segments. This was a complete product and go-to-market exercise — touching copy, UX, information architecture, and the signup funnel end to end.
