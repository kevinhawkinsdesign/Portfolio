— Overview

Monta is Europe's leading EV charging platform. I joined as the first UX leader at the Director/VP level — hired to formalize design practice, scale the team, and connect UX work directly to product growth.

— Challenge

What we inherited

Monta had talented individual contributors but no shared design language, no research practice, and no organizational infrastructure for UX to operate at scale. Our job was to build all of that — fast — without slowing down the product teams already shipping.

— Approach

How we built it

We started with the team. I hired across design, research, and content — building a 20+ person org with clear levels, career frameworks, and ways of working that didn't exist before. Every hire was deliberate: we needed range, not just seniority.

In parallel, I led the development of our design system and a centralized research practice — two investments that compounded quickly. Within three months, teams were shipping with more consistency and learning from users at a pace they hadn't achieved before.

I also personally led the design of Monta Hub AI — our AI-powered operator intelligence layer — as a proof of concept for what the practice could produce when it operated at its best.

— Reflection

What I learned

The hardest part of first-in-role leadership isn't building the thing — it's building trust while building the thing. You're being evaluated constantly while operating without precedent. The answer is to lead by example early, delegate fast, and let the team's output speak for the org's value.
