Context

Designing across a travel empire

Booking Holdings is one of the world's largest travel companies — operating Booking.com, Kayak, Priceline, Agoda, OpenTable, and Rentalcars.com as distinct but strategically connected platforms. Our role at Booking.com was to lead design for Connected Stay and cross-brand initiatives: product experiences that bridged multiple platforms into a coherent, intelligent traveller journey.

This was a uniquely complex design challenge. Each platform had its own design system, user base, and product culture — yet the opportunity to create genuine value for travellers who were already using multiple Booking Holdings products was enormous. Our team operated at the intersection of product vision, technical constraint, and cross-brand politics.

Key Feature · Shipped

Group Trip Planner — from hackathon to production

One of the clearest demonstrations of what happens when designers are given autonomy, speed, and cross-functional buy-in was the Group Trip Planner. We conceived, prototyped, and validated this feature during an internal hackathon — designing a collaborative trip-planning experience that allowed groups to plan, vote on options, and book together within a single Booking.com flow.

The feature addressed a genuine pain point: group travel coordination typically required multiple tools, endless message threads, and someone volunteering to be the "planner." Our solution surfaced personalised recommendations, let each traveller indicate preferences, and unified the booking decision into a single streamlined experience. The hackathon prototype was polished and shipped to production — one of the more satisfying full-cycle moments in the engagement.

Key Feature · Shipped

Local Events Hotel Search — context-first discovery

The second hackathon win was Local Events Hotel Search: a feature that reframed the accommodation search paradigm around events rather than dates. Instead of "I need a hotel in Amsterdam, Feb 24–26," the experience became "Beyoncé is performing at Johan Cruyff Arena on Feb 24 — here are the best places to stay nearby, with contextual neighbourhood information, canal tours, and local attractions."

This was a bet on context-first discovery — surfacing accommodation at moments of high intent rather than waiting for users to initiate a search. The feature drew on Booking.com's rich data infrastructure to surface personalized, location-aware recommendations tied to concerts, sports events, festivals, and cultural moments in key cities.

Key Feature

Connected Stay — bridging the Booking Holdings ecosystem

The broader Connected Stay initiative was about creating intelligent continuity across a traveller's entire journey — not just the accommodation booking, but the discovery, planning, and on-trip experience. We worked across the sister brands (Kayak for flight search, OpenTable for dining, Rentalcars for ground transport) to design shared data models, surface relevant cross-platform recommendations, and reduce the fragmentation that forced travellers to context-switch between apps.

This work required as much organisational design as product design — navigating platform autonomy, competing roadmaps, and the natural tension between local optimization and cross-brand coherence. We learned as much about leading across organisational lines as we did about designing multi-platform experiences.
