Context

The situation

Following Glovo's acquisition by Delivery Hero, we needed to accelerate growth across B2C and B2B products in 25 markets. The UX org was fragmented — different teams, no unified research practice, and design functioning more as a production service than a strategic partner.

Approach

What we built

We rebuilt the org from the inside — unifying standards, tooling, and processes while preserving team speed. I led two company-wide hackathons with the CPO and CEO to rapidly iterate on homepage, order tracking, social shopping, and loyalty program features.

We personalized CRM and promotions, boosting new customer acquisition by 15% and retention by 20% while preserving LTV. A universal font replacement project saved €500k EUR annually — a direct P&L contribution. We also built localization as an in-house agency supporting 19 languages with 24-hour turnaround.

Reflection

What I learned

Running a 100-person org across 25 markets taught me that the most important thing a design leader can do isn't design — it's create clarity. Clear ownership, clear standards, clear connection to business outcomes.
