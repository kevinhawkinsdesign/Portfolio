The situation

Amenitiz had strong product-market fit in its core European markets but needed to scale internationally — across 60+ countries and 15,000+ properties — without degrading the experience that made it successful. There was no research practice, no localization framework, and no dedicated design leadership. I was brought in to build those foundations and lead the product transformation required to support international scale.

What we built

We recruited a full team of six designers in the first 45 days — defining roles and standards, running a tight hiring process, and onboarding people into an environment without established design systems or documentation. Rather than waiting until the team was settled, we launched immediately into an innovation workshop with Product and Engineering to align on priorities and build shared context fast.

Before touching the product, I led three field research trips to key accounts — visiting properties directly to understand how hospitality operators actually work across different markets. That research informed everything that followed and gave the team a shared grounding in user reality that analytics alone can't provide.

Core UI redesigns

We redesigned Amenitiz's three most critical UIs: calendar, self-service onboarding, and inventory management — the surfaces that determined whether a new property could get up and running independently. The redesigns were built to scale across languages, property types, and operational contexts, enabling the international expansion without requiring localised support.

I personally led the Brazilian market launch: new language, personalized product experience, and a streamlined self-service signup flow designed for a lower-LTV prospect profile and a full PLG motion. No sales-assisted onboarding — the product had to do the work on its own.

What I learned

Moving fast in a new role doesn't mean skipping the research. The three field trips to customer accounts were the single best investment of those early months — surfacing problems no analytics dashboard would have shown, and building instant credibility with product and engineering teams who saw that design was willing to get its hands dirty.

Outcomes

+20% LTV & retention — NPS held flat through the transition
6 designers hired and onboarded in first 45 days
60+ countries supported via redesigned core UIs
1 new market launched (Brazil) with full PLG motion
