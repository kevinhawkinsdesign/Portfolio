BookClub · Director of Product Design · Oct 2020–Feb 2022

Building a social reading platform from zero as founding designer

Role
Director of Product Design
Scope
Full product — 0 to 1
Focus
Community · Author engagement · Reading UX

The situation

BookClub brings authors and readers together via online book clubs, led by the authors themselves — and sometimes celebrity hosts. Authors guide readers through their books with public discussions, video series, Q&As, and the opportunity to have an author join a personal reading group.

I joined as the founding designer and scaled to Director of Product Design — responsible for the entire product design and user research function from day one.

What we built

Starting from scratch means making every foundational decision: information architecture, design system, interaction patterns, research methodology, and the team itself. We built a product that had to feel intimate and community-driven at any scale — which is a fundamentally different design challenge than optimizing an existing product for growth metrics.

The core design challenge was making asynchronous community feel synchronous — giving readers the feeling of shared experience even when they're reading independently, and giving authors a stage without making the product feel like a broadcast medium.

What I learned

Zero-to-one product work is humbling in the best way. There's no existing data to validate decisions, no user base to run tests against, and no established patterns to reference. The most valuable skill isn't any particular design method — it's the judgment to know which decisions matter enough to validate and which ones you just have to commit to and learn from.
